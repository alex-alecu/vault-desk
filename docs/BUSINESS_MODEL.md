# Business Model

Created: 2026-07-10

Vault Desk should create trust and distribution through a useful free community product, then sell certainty through support, hardware, business controls, installation, and professional workflow deployment.

## Community Software

The community application should be:

- Free and open source.
- Useful for personal and commercial use.
- Local and offline by default.
- Not artificially limited by document count, messages, or basic usage.

Revenue can come from:

- Optional one-off support incidents.
- Optional annual support plans.
- Installation help.
- Hardware compatibility support.

No payment should be required for continued basic operation.

## Business Control Layer

A separate closed-source layer may provide:

- Multi-user administration.
- Identity and permissions.
- Policies and approvals.
- Audit records.
- Shared workspaces.
- Backup and disaster recovery.
- Appliance administration.
- Fleet management.
- Long-term supported releases.
- Business integrations.
- Support SLAs.

This layer should be modular so it can support the office appliance and later managed personal systems.

## Ownership Principle

A purchased version should continue working permanently.

Renewals should pay for:

- Support.
- Newer versions.
- Security updates.
- Workflow improvements.
- Certified model and runtime updates.
- Business integrations.

Renewals should not be framed as permission to access the customer's existing working system.

## Hardware Revenue

Hardware buyers pay to avoid:

- Hardware research.
- Installation.
- Model selection.
- Runtime configuration.
- Security setup.
- Benchmarking.
- Maintenance.
- Fragmented support.

Revenue sources:

- Hardware margin.
- Configuration and validation.
- Installation.
- Data migration.
- Initial onboarding.
- Extended replacement coverage.
- Annual support.
- Business control software.
- Integrations.
- Professional workflow deployment.

## Positioning

Do not sell "local AI" as the complete value proposition. Sell:

- Known hardware.
- Validated models.
- Safe workflows.
- Central controls.
- Clear support boundaries.
- Customer ownership.
- Measurable time savings.
